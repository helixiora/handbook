## Public-facing information architecture

This site works best when it acts like a compact knowledge product rather than a generic documentation portal. Public readers do not need every internal detail. They need a clean narrative.

The recommended page types are:

- **Orientation pages** for context, positioning, and scope.
- **Method pages** for working practices and delivery rhythm.
- **Standards pages** for tone, writing rules, and visual consistency.
- **Reference pages** for links, reusable snippets, and publishing mechanics.

## Navigation rules

The top navigation should stay thin. A public handbook benefits from confidence, not from menu sprawl.

| Keep | Avoid |
| --- | --- |
| 3 to 5 primary links | Large nested menus |
| Strong section landing pages | Empty category pages |
| One clear CTA | Multiple competing buttons |

## Section design

Each section landing page should answer three questions quickly:

1. Why does this section exist?
2. Which pages matter most?
3. What should the reader do next?

This sample Hugo site answers those questions through page leads, card summaries, and a short related-pages rail in the article layout.

## Content that belongs here

Good public handbook topics for Helixiora might include:

- Point of view on AI, data, and platform work.
- Delivery principles for client engagements.
- Publishing standards for public articles and notes.
- Technical operating practices that differentiate the company.

The test is simple: if publishing the page improves trust, clarity, or understanding, it belongs here.

## What this page is for

This appendix captures Netherlands-specific employment setup, holiday handling, and contract terms that differ from the global baseline.

## What you should do

Use this page to confirm:

- which contract types we use in the Netherlands
- how Dutch public holidays are handled
- sick leave treatment by contract form
- internship baseline terms
- commuting support and pension position

## Employment forms in the Netherlands

We employ in the Netherlands through these forms:

1. Full-time staff on a permanent contract (temporary contracts may be used for specific positions, depending on role requirements).
2. 0-hours contracts.
3. Internships with a monthly internship fee of EUR 500.

## Sick leave by contract type

- 0-hours contract: if you are sick, there is no pay for those hours.
- Contract-based employment (including full-time staff): sick leave remains paid in line with the contract.

For background on call-based contracts (including 0-hours structures), see the Dutch government guidance on [oproepcontracten](https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/onderwerpen/arbeidsovereenkomst-en-cao/vraag-en-antwoord/wat-is-een-oproepcontract).

## Internship terms

Internships are normally 6 months. Final duration can be adjusted based on your school's requirements and should be agreed before the internship starts.
